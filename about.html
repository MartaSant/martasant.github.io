About Food Facts

Who is behind Food Facts, what this project is about, and how articles are researched and written.

1. What is Food Facts?

Food Facts is a quiet corner of the internet dedicated to how everyday foods are produced, processed, labelled and sometimes overproduced. It looks at food not only as something on your plate, but as the result of long chains of decisions: from fields and factories to logistics, marketing, regulation and waste.

The goal is simple: to help you understand the story behind what you buy, cook and eat, so you can make more informed choices that respect your health, your budget and the people and resources involved in food production.

3. How articles are written

Food systems are complex, and it‚Äôs easy to get lost between marketing claims, strong opinions and scary headlines. To keep a steady line, articles on Food Facts follow a simple methodology:

  • Start from real products and processes — posts often begin with a very concrete question: how is this specific food made, what does this label mean, what happens when there is a surplus of a certain product?
  • Look at multiple sources — scientific papers, EU regulations, technical documents, industry reports, and qualitative sources (such as interviews and field experience) are considered together.
  • Avoid ‚Äúgood vs bad‚Äù shortcuts — instead of treating ‚Äúindustrial‚Äù and ‚Äúhomemade‚Äù as automatic opposites, the focus is on conditions, trade-offs and practical context.
  • Translate without oversimplifying — whenever technical terms are used, they are explained in plain language, but nuances are kept instead of forcing everything into black-and-white answers.
  • Connect back to daily life — theory is linked to what you actually see in shops: labels, price differences, promotions, formats and waste at home.

5. How to use this blog

You don‚Äôt need to read Food Facts as if it were a textbook. Think of it more like a curious friend who loves both food and data and shares what she finds out, calmly and with references.
