Privacy & data use

How Food Facts handles email addresses, messages and other information shared through the website.

1. Who runs this website

Food Facts is a personal project run by Marta, based in Italy. The website is hosted on GitHub Pages and uses third-party services to handle contact forms and the newsletter.

This page is meant to explain in clear, simple language how data is used on this site. It is not legal advice and may be updated over time as the project grows.

2. What data is collected

2.1. Messages sent via the contact form

When you send a message through the contact form on the Contact page, the following information may be collected:

  • the content of your message;
  • any name or email address you choose to include in the message body;
  • technical information automatically collected by the form provider (such as IP address, browser type and time of submission).

Messages are sent via Formspree and delivered to Marta’s email inbox so that she can read and answer them.

2.2. Newsletter subscriptions

When you subscribe to the Food Facts newsletter, you provide:

  • your email address;
  • optionally, other information you decide to share in the form.

Newsletter subscriptions are managed through MailerLite (or a similar email service provider). This service also collects technical information such as:

  • which newsletters you open or don’t open;
  • whether you click on links inside emails;
  • approximate location based on IP address and other standard email analytics.

2.3. Technical logs and hosting

The site is hosted on GitHub Pages. Like most hosting providers, GitHub may collect server logs and technical data (such as IP address and browser information) to keep the service secure and running smoothly. This is handled directly by GitHub according to their own policies.

2.4. No extra tracking added by Marta

At this time, no additional analytics tools (such as Google Analytics) or advertising trackers have been intentionally added to Food Facts. If this ever changes, this page will be updated to explain what is used and why.

3. How your data is used

Data collected through the site is used only for a few specific purposes:

  • To reply to your messages – when you contact Marta through the form, your message and any contact details you provide are used only to read, understand and answer your request.
  • To send the newsletter – your email address is used to send Food Facts updates, new articles and related content, only if you have subscribed to the newsletter.
  • To keep the site functioning – hosting and email service providers may use technical data (logs, IP addresses, etc.) to protect against abuse, ensure security and improve their services.

Marta does not sell, rent or trade your email address or personal data to third parties for marketing purposes.

4. Where your data is stored

Depending on how you interact with Food Facts, your data may be stored in:

  • Email inbox – messages sent via the contact form are delivered by Formspree to Marta’s email account.
  • Newsletter platform – email addresses and newsletter-related data (subscriptions, unsubscriptions, open/click statistics) are stored securely in MailerLite (or a similar provider).
  • Service provider logs – hosting and form services (GitHub, Formspree, MailerLite) may keep technical logs on their own servers.

These providers have their own privacy policies and security measures. Food Facts relies on them to process and store data safely on Marta’s behalf.

5. How long data is kept

  • Contact messages – messages may be kept in the email inbox for as long as they are useful to remember past conversations or to improve content. Marta may occasionally clean old messages that are no longer needed.
  • Newsletter data – your email address stays in the mailing list until you unsubscribe or the list is cleaned. Basic statistics about past campaigns may remain in the email platform, but they are used only in aggregate (for example, to understand which topics readers find helpful).

6. Your choices & rights

You are always free to:

  • Unsubscribe from the newsletter – using the unsubscribe link provided by the email service in each newsletter you receive.
  • Ask to have your data removed – for example, you can ask Marta to delete your email address from the mailing list or remove past messages, where possible.
  • Choose how much you share – in the contact form, you are free to share only the information you feel comfortable sharing.

To request changes or deletion of your data, you can:

  • use the contact form and clearly describe your request, or
  • reply directly to one of the newsletters you received.

7. Cookies and tracking

Food Facts does not intentionally use additional analytics or advertising cookies beyond what is technically needed for the site to function and for the newsletter to be delivered.

However, third-party services involved in hosting or sending email (for example GitHub, Formspree or MailerLite) may use their own cookies or similar technologies according to their privacy policies. Food Facts does not control how those third-party cookies work.

8. Updates to this page

This privacy & data page may be updated from time to time, for example if new features are added to the site or if the tools used to manage messages and newsletters change.

When changes are relevant, a short note may be added to this page or mentioned in a newsletter so that regular readers can stay informed.
